Shear Viscosity in $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM Plasma Through the AdS/CFT Correspondence

Riccardo Finotello

This talk aims at the preparation of the seminar for the course Introduction to the Physics of the Quark-Gluon Plasma of the Doctoral School. In the first part of the seminar we build the basic tools needed for the analysis, namely we build the AdS/CFT correspondence as presented by Maldacena: starting from basic knowledge of General Relativity, we build a SUGRA theory containing black holes and branes, show the equivalence of p-branes and Dp-branes from string theory and define the AdS/CFT duality. After showing a simple application in the computation of the inter-quark potential, we apply the procedure to compute the shear viscosity of a viscous plasma at finite temperature.

Measurement of Higgs boson properties in the $4\ell$ final state at $\sqrt{s}=13~\mathrm{TeV}$ at CMS

Alessandra Cappati

Since its discovery in 2012 by the ATLAS and CMS Collaborations at the CERN LHC, the Higgs boson has been deeply studied in order to understand its nature in detail and to test possible discrepancies from the Standard Model of particle physics. Among its different possible decay modes, the Higgs boson to four lepton decay channel ($\mathrm{H} \to \mathrm{ZZ} \to 4\ell$) is one of the most important. It has been widely exploited for the Higgs boson discovery and for the measurement of its properties, thanks to the large signal over background ratio and to the possibility of fully reconstructing the kinematics of the decay. This talk presents the status of the analysis on Higgs boson properties measured in the $\mathrm{H} \to \mathrm{ZZ} \to 4\ell$ decay channel using proton-proton collision data collected by the CMS experiment at a centre-of-mass energy of 13 TeV. In particular, the measurement of the signal strength modifier (defined as the ratio of the observed Higgs boson rate in the $\mathrm{H} \to \mathrm{ZZ} \to 4\ell$ channel to the Standard Model expectation) is given both inclusively and separately for the individual Higgs boson production modes.
